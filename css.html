RSS

Rashtriya Swayamsevak Sangh

Rashtriya Swayamsevak Sangh (RSS, lit. 'National Volunteer Union' or 'National Volunteer Corps') is an Indian right-wing Hindutva volunteer paramilitary organisation. It is the progenitor and leader of a large body of Hindutva organisations called the Sangh Parivar, which includes the Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP), the ruling political party under Narendra Modi. Mohan Bhagwat currently serves as the Sarsanghchalak (Chief) of the RSS, with Dattatreya Hosabale as the Sarkaryavah (General Secretary).

Founded on 27 September 1925, the organisation aimed to provide character training and instil "Hindu discipline" to unite the Hindu community and establish a Hindu Rashtra (Hindu nation). The RSS has been banned multiple times in history and is one of the world’s largest organisations by membership.

Founding
The RSS was founded in 1925 by K. B. Hedgewar, a doctor from Nagpur, British India. Hedgewar was influenced by Vinayak Damodar Savarkar's pamphlet "Essentials of Hindutva" and sought to strengthen Hindu society. He recruited Hindu youth, gave them uniforms, and taught them paramilitary techniques with lathis (bamboo sticks) and swords. The saffron flag, Bhagwa Dhwaj, became the organisation's emblem.
